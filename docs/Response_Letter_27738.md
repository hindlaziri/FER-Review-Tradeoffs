We would like to thank the reviewers and the editorial team for their constructive and insightful comments. We have carefully considered all the suggestions and have revised the manuscript accordingly. We believe that these revisions have significantly improved the quality, clarity, and scientific rigor of our paper.

Below, we provide a point-by-point response to each comment raised by the reviewers.

---

## Response to Reviewer A

**1. Title**
*Comment: The current title suggests an experimental comparative study, whereas the actual content indicates a literature-based (conceptual) review. Recommendation: Revise the title to clearly reflect that the paper is a review article.*
**Response:** We agree with the reviewer. The title has been revised to: "A Comprehensive Review of Conventional and Deep Learning Approaches for Facial Emotion Recognition: Performance, Datasets, and Trade-offs" to accurately reflect the nature of the study.

**2. Abstract**
*Comment: The abstract is too general, lacks evidence, does not state the type of study, and the unique contribution is not clearly stated.*
**Response:** The abstract has been completely rewritten. It now explicitly states that this is a comprehensive review, highlights the specific contribution (structured comparison focusing on trade-offs between accuracy, robustness, and computational cost), and includes mentions of the datasets analyzed.

**3. Introduction**
*Comment: The introduction is too long, overly narrative, lacks a clear research gap, and the novelty/motivation are not clearly articulated.*
**Response:** We have condensed the general background on emotions and added a dedicated subsection "Research Gap and Contribution" that explicitly outlines the limitations of previous surveys and the specific objectives of our paper.

**4. Theoretical Foundation and Review Methodology**
*Comment: There is no explanation of how the literature was selected. Add a short section titled “Methodology of Literature Review”.*
**Response:** We have added a new section "Methodology of Literature Review" detailing the databases used, keywords, inclusion criteria, and the time range of publications (primarily 2019-2025).

**5. Analysis and Discussion**
*Comment: No quantitative data or numerical comparison is provided. Common FER datasets are not discussed in depth. Figures are too small.*
**Response:** We have significantly enriched this section. We added a new subsection "FER Datasets Overview" with a table detailing CK+, JAFFE, FER2013, RAF-DB, and AffectNet. We also updated the comparative table to include typical accuracy ranges on FER2013 and CK+, as well as computational costs. Figures have been enlarged for better readability.

**6. Experimental Evaluation**
*Comment: The paper claims to be a comparative study, but no experimental evaluation is presented. Recommendation: Add experiments or clearly position as a review article.*
**Response:** We have added a new section "Experimental Comparison" where we trained and evaluated a conventional method (LBP + SVM) and a deep learning method (Basic CNN) on the standard FER2013 dataset. We have included confusion matrices, training curves, and a detailed comparison table showing accuracy, F1-score, and computational time to provide concrete empirical evidence supporting our comparative analysis.

**7. Conclusion**
*Comment: No discussion of study limitations. Lack of critical reflection. Future research directions are generic. No citations in conclusion.*
**Response:** The conclusion has been revised to explicitly acknowledge the limitations of the study (literature-based nature). We also provided more specific future research directions (e.g., lightweight CNNs for edge computing, addressing demographic biases). Citations have been removed from the conclusion as per journal guidelines.

**8. References and Citations**
*Comment: Citations are not sequential. Some references are not cited. Increase the number of references (recommended ≥ 25). Follow IEEE format.*
**Response:** We have thoroughly revised the references. The number of references has been increased to 25, focusing on recent journal articles (2020-2025). All citations are now sequential, properly cited in the text, and strictly follow the IEEE format.

## Response to Reviewer B

**1. Introduction Structure**
*Comment: Rewrite the Introduction with a clear logical flow (context → problem → state of the art → gap → contribution → significance).*
**Response:** The introduction has been restructured exactly as recommended, with clear subsections guiding the reader from the context to the specific contributions of the paper.

**2. Method and Datasets**
*Comment: Strengthen the Method with dataset details, preprocessing justification, and reproducibility.*
**Response:** We have added a detailed table and discussion on common FER datasets (size, environment, characteristics). The preprocessing pipeline has been elaborated with specific examples (e.g., MTCNN for face detection).

**3. Results & Discussion**

**4. Limitations and Future Work**
*Comment: Acknowledge dataset bias, need for multimodal fusion, and computational resource constraints. Suggest specific future work.*
**Response:** These points have been explicitly integrated into the revised Conclusion and Discussion sections.
